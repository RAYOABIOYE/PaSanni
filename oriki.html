ORIKI MOLEBI SANNI ABIOYE (LINEAGE PRAISE POEM OF SANNI ABIOYE CLAN)

The Descendant of Alaaye
Who works to wealth
The restlessly enterprising
The boldly brave
A personification of fetishism
Cherishing Oyo, vowing never to return home
The calamity of Iloko
Mandated to secure his in-law's domain
Anticipating the kind-hearted
But, the kind-hearted emerges not
Yet, my legacy sword returns not into its sheath idly
Descendant of the Iloko Lord
Who cheerfully confronts death
Homeless shall the opulent be on the outbreak of war
The Iloko is invoked to die
He sits in splendor, legs stretched out
He seeks to know how to die
He that kills to yield a feast of meals for all
He that murders to yield blood drinks for all
Iloko, the calamity of Iloko
The Possessor of the sharp sword
Whose sword spares not the in-law
Who uses the sword to slay his in-law
Declaring his in-law worthy of brutal killing
The Descendant of Olofa Mojo
The Descendant of the Olalomi
That shares universal admiration with the yam
Descendant of Ijesa that sees not the need for matches
From home does the Owa slave convey fire unto the farm
The Owner of the only free-for-all tree at Owa Square
Which singular leaf, when plucked, is worth Four-Hundred
Pluck two, Eight-Hundred
Pluck three, Twelve-Hundred
Pluck four, Sixteen-Hundred
Pluck five, Twenty-Hundred
Pluck six, Twenty-Four-Hundred
Pluck seven, Twenty-Eight-Hundred
Pluck eight, Thirty-Two-Hundred
Pluck nine, Thirty-Four-Hundred
Pluck ten, Thirty-Eight-Hundred
Esu it was that afflicted me that I plucked ten
And, I became enslaved to Owa
Wrestling, the festive ritual of Ofa
Hero of the epic battle in Ofa, the abode Olalomi
Which witness was sought
Witnessed it was by leaves in the farm
Witnessed it was by farm heaps
If present, the farm lord would have brokered peace
The hero of justice and equity
Inequity dare not manifest in Ofa
Should injustice manifest in Ofa
The royal war is it that conquers the clan of such mongers
Never has my clan been war-conquered

The Sanni Abioye Family has its root in the Ajigbayin Family of Oke-Iremo, Ile Ife, the historical source of the Yoruba Race. In totality, Sanni had SIXTEEN CHILDREN
